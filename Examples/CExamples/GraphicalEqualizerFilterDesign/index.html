SigLib Graphical Equalizer Filter Design Example Program

  

The files in this folder provide examples of how to design a filter bank for a graphical equalizer.

Graphical equalizers and tone control filters employ a series of cascaded Infinite Impulse Response (IIR) filters, each consisting of a single biquad per frequency band.
These filter banks are commonly distributed uniformly across the logarithmic frequency spectrum to emulate the octave-based characteristics of music and the human auditory system.
The illustration below depicts this arrangement.



The filter bank design process involves computing the center frequencies and bandwidths for every filter, followed by the careful alignment of filter band edges (-3 dB points) to eliminate any potential overlaps or gaps between the bands.
It's important to highlight that the conversion of filter parameters from the logarithmic (octave) frequency domain to the linear frequency domain is essential, as demonstrated in the previously mentioned diagram.
In this context, the application selects the logarithmic midpoint between two adjacent filter bank center frequencies as the designated cross-over point.

The filter bank is a multi-dimensional array that includes entries for the number of filter bands and the number of gain levels for each filter band.

The filter bands are:

  • low-shelf - The low-shelf filter
  • (N-2) * peaking - The peaking filters
  • high-shelf - The high-shelf filter
This code is based on the equations in Robert Bristow-Johnson's Audio Equalizer Cookbook and includes example configurations for 3, 5 and 10 band equalizers.
